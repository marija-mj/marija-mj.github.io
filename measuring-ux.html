Measuring UX at Cloud Watch

Establishing a scalable UX measurement framework that transformed feedback into actionable product decisions.


Product / Company: CloudWatch (AWS)

My role: Senior UX Designer

Timeframe: 6 months in 2022

Team size: 5 product and dev teams

Product type: B2B enterprise platform (AWS cloud monitoring and observability service)

Scale: Service with 3 million active account and 5 product areas

The Problem / Opportunity

CloudWatch relied primarily on aggregate NPS and sentiment feedback as UX KPIs. As the platform expanded across multiple services and workflows, these metrics became too broad to be actionable. I recognized the opportunity to shift from reactive sentiment tracking to a systematic, workflow-level UX measurement framework.

Key challenges
  • No visibility into which services or workflows were underperforming
  • Feedback skewed negative and lacked context
  • No automated or scalable measurement infrastructure
  • Limited development capacity to support new instrumentation

Scope and responsibilities

  • Decision Authority - Owned the end-to-end UX measurement strategy, including defining success criteria, selecting methodologies, prioritizing services, and preparing proposal materials for quarterly planning.
  • Project Ownership - Operated without dedicated PM support, managing planning, timelines, deliverables, and cross-team coordination alongside hands-on design and research execution.
  • Cross-Team Involvement - Partnered with five product teams and engineering counterparts to map core workflows and implement UI tagging required for analytics instrumentation.
  • Research Ownership - Designed surveys, recruited participants, conducted 5 customer interviews, and synthesized findings into a UX health model combining ease of use, usefulness, visual appeal, and information clarity.

How I drove the initiative

  • Built early alignment and secured prioritization by pre-socializing a strategic proposal with key stakeholders before quarterly planning, iterating on feedback to emphasize actionable deliverables and a clear scaling roadmap.
  • Defined a scalable UX measurement framework combining UX health criteria (ease of use, usefulness, visual appeal, information clarity), task success rates, and automated feedback loops—balancing depth of insight with long-term sustainability.
  • Facilitated cross-team workflow mapping workshops with five high-traffic service teams to identify 5–7 critical user tasks per service and uncover cross-service journeys, creating a shared understanding of priorities and dependencies.
  • Unlocked engineering capacity by reducing implementation friction, personally learning the UI tagging system and translating measurement requirements into lightweight development tasks, shortening delivery timelines.
  • Established a repeatable collaboration model that enabled teams to continue measuring UX independently after launch, embedding measurement practices into ongoing roadmap planning rather than treating them as a one-off initiative

Key decissions and trade-offs

  • Prioritized Top 3 Services Over Full Coverage - Initial scope included ~10 services and dozens of workflows, which proved unsustainable. Narrowing to the three highest-traffic services enabled faster validation and demonstrable value within a single quarter.
  • Dual Measurement Model - Chose a hybrid of manual surveys and automated feedback whch gave less granular perfection in exchange for long-term scalability and sustainability.
  • Pivoted Research Scope Mid-Project - Recruitment challenges led to reducing planned interviews from 5 per service to 5 total, focusing only on the most painful workflows identified through survey data to maximize insight per call.
  • Expanded Scope to Include Analytics Infrastructure - While defining the strategy, discovered a lack of accessible click/funnel analytics. Added UI tagging and dashboard implementation to the proposal to enable long-term visibility and decision-making.

Outcomes

  • Established a repeatable UX health measurement framework enabling continuous monitoring
  • Identified critical usability gaps across top services using mixed-method research and delivered 5 prioritized improvement proposals for the next development cycle
  • Implemented UI tagging infrastructure enabling click and funnel analytics
  • Created a roadmap for scaling measurement across the broader organization

Impact

  • 5 services instrumented measurement for 5 main workflows doubling product usage insights
  • Increased UX health score across 5 main workflows by 3.5% in the following 2 quarters

Reflections and learnings

  • Pre-socializing strategic proposals increases success rates by addressing concerns before formal decision points.
  • Thinking beyond the immediate task can uncover opportunities for systemic impact — identifying analytics gaps led to organization-wide benefits.
  • Actionable deliverables matter more than research alone when securing leadership buy-in.
